My Thought Process

Discovery: Meeting with the Client

The discovery phase is crucial for creating a successful website for your client. During the meeting with your client, ask about their brand style guide and logo, and suggest creating one if they don't have it. Ask if they have any pegs or ideas for the website, and what kind of functions they need, such as e-commerce, blog, or contact form. By gathering this information, you'll be able to create a website that meets their requirements and exceeds their expectations.

Website Strategy: Sitemap, Wireframes, and Web Design

The website strategy phase is critical for effective website development. This phase involves creating a sitemap to organize the website's structure, wireframes to plan the layout and content, and web design to establish the website's visual identity in line with the client's brand. By carefully strategizing and designing the website, the final product will not only meet the client's needs but also deliver an outstanding user experience, driving more traffic, leads, and conversions to the website.

Development: Styleguide, Environment, Building, and QA

In the development phase, the team creates a style guide to ensure consistency, sets up the website environment, builds the website, and conducts rigorous QA to ensure its proper functioning and alignment with the client's objectives. With a well-developed website, the client can provide an exceptional user experience, drive traffic, increase conversions, and meet their business goals.
